How to prepare Nigerian Jollof Rice


The Nigerian Jollof Rice is popular all over the World for it's spicy taste and close competitor; Ghana jollof.
The Nigerian jollof is quite an easy dish to prepare, although there are different ways it can be prepared basically varying based on culture, But today we'll be trying out one of my grandma's famous recipe.

Ingredients

  • Rice (boiled)
  • Fresh Tomatoes (Blended and boiled dry)
  • Fresh Pepper (Blended and boiled dry)
  • Fresh tatashe (Blended and boiled dry)
  • Tomatoe puree
  • Onions (neatly sliced)
  • Meat (Preferrably beef)
  • Egg (boiled)
  • Garlic and ginger (grinded)
  • Groundnut Oil
  • Seasoning cube
  • Salt
  • Curry
  • Thyme
  • Onga
  • Stock
  • Water

  • 2 cups
  • 7 medium balls
  • 5 large balls

  • 1 large bulb
  • 8 medium pieces
  • 3 medium sizes

  • To taste
  • 1 sachet

Method
  1. Heat up a pan on low heat and pour in your groundnut oil and wait for it to heat up as well
  2. Then add your chopped onions and allow to fry a minute, then put in all your blended and boiled dry ingredients; fresh tomatoes, fresh pepper and fresh tatashe and fry for 3 minutes while stirring in between.
  3. Then add you Tomatoe puree with garlic and ginger and keep frying until tanginess is off
  4. Keep frying until almost dry then pour in your stock, add ur seasoning cubes, curry, thyme, onga and salt.
  5. Allow to boil before add your water and also allow to boil the put your already parboiled rice and allow to cook.
  6. Make sure to keep checking on it and when the water is dried up, then add your fried meat and eggs too; Allow to simmer for 3 minutes
  7. When it's simmered for 3 mins the dish the rice with a chilled Juice (Preferrably Orange juice ) or Yoghurt.
